About Bubbley

Bubbley analyzes meeting audio in real-time to reveal how well your team is sharing the conversation. Place your iPhone on the table, and watch as the app tracks speaking time, turn-taking balance, and meeting health — all instantly, all locally.

The problem

In meetings, some voices dominate while others fade into silence. You might not realize it until afterward, and by then the moment has passed. Hybrid meetings are even harder to read — remote participants on Zoom sound like one anonymous voice. Traditional meeting notes don't capture participation patterns.

How Bubbley works

Bubbley uses on-device machine learning to listen to meeting audio and extract three key insights:

  • Speaking time — How long each participant spoke (in seconds and percentages)
  • Turn-taking balance — How well the group shares speaking turns (higher scores mean more balanced, natural back-and-forth)
  • Meeting health score — A composite measure of participation equity and conversation flow

Key features

  • Real-time analysis — Watch the metrics update as the meeting unfolds
  • Participant detection — Automatically identifies distinct speakers (both in-person and remote)
  • Turn-taking breakdown — See how many times each person spoke and how the conversation flowed
  • Session recording — Save meeting summaries for later review and team debrief
  • Zero cloud dependency — All analysis happens on your device. Audio is never uploaded.
  • Works with hybrid meetings — Captures both in-person voices and remote participants playing through your speaker
  • Simple, distraction-free UI — All the insights you need without unnecessary complexity

How to use

  1. Open Bubbley and tap Start Meeting
  2. Place your iPhone on the table in the middle of the conference room (or anywhere you can hear all speakers clearly)
  3. For remote participants, play audio through your Mac/laptop speaker (not headphones) so your iPhone's mic picks it up
  4. Start your meeting — Bubbley will begin analyzing in the background
  5. After the meeting ends, tap Stop to see the full analysis
  6. Review speaking time, turn-taking balance, and overall meeting health
  7. Optionally save the session for future reference or team discussion

Who should use Bubbley?

  • Meeting facilitators — Ensure all voices are heard and balanced
  • Team leads — Identify participation patterns and psychological safety issues
  • DEI advocates — Measure and improve equitable speaking time
  • Research teams — Study conversational dynamics and group behavior
  • Anyone who wants better meetings — Get objective data on what actually happened

Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-04-26

This document explains what information the iOS app Bubbley ("the App") accesses, what it does with that information, and what it does not do.

1) Audio processing — local only

The App uses your iPhone's microphone to listen to meeting audio. All audio processing happens on your device using on-device machine learning models. The raw audio is never transmitted, uploaded, or stored on external servers.

The App extracts only the following metrics from audio:

  • Speaking time per participant (in seconds)
  • Number of speaking turns
  • Turn-taking balance scores
  • Overall meeting health metrics

These derived metrics are what you see in the app. The original audio is not retained after analysis is complete.

2) Data you explicitly save

When you end a meeting analysis, the App offers to optionally save the session summary to your device. This summary includes:

  • Session date and duration
  • List of detected speakers (no identifying information beyond "Speaker 1, Speaker 2", etc.)
  • Speaking time for each speaker
  • Meeting health metrics

This data is stored locally on your device only in the app's sandbox. It is never transmitted externally unless you explicitly export or share it.

3) Data we do not collect

The App does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any of the following:

  • Personal identifiable information (name, email, phone, etc.)
  • Meeting content or transcripts
  • Raw audio files
  • Calendar or contact data
  • Analytics or usage telemetry
  • Advertising tracking data

4) Permissions explained

PermissionReason
NSMicrophoneUsageDescriptionRecord meeting audio for on-device analysis

No other permissions are requested or used.

5) Machine learning models

The App uses two on-device machine learning models:

  • LS-EEND (Local Speaker Extraction and End-to-End Diarization) — Identifies distinct speakers from audio
  • Silero VAD (Voice Activity Detection) — Detects speech vs. silence to identify turn boundaries

Both models run entirely on your device and do not transmit audio or data externally. The models are bundled with the app and are not updated or communicated with external services.

6) No third-party services

The App does not send audio, metrics, or any other data to third-party services, analytics providers, or cloud platforms. There are no external API calls related to your meeting data or audio processing.

7) Microphone access

The App requires microphone access to function. You can revoke this permission at any time in Settings > Privacy > Microphone. Once revoked, the app will not work until you grant it again.

8) Data retention on your device

Session summaries are stored on your iPhone until you delete them. You can delete any saved session directly from the app. If you uninstall the app, all locally stored data is automatically deleted by iOS.

9) Exporting or sharing sessions

If you choose to export or share a session summary (e.g., email a PDF to your team), you are responsible for that sharing decision. The App does not force or automatically export any data.

10) Children's privacy

The App is not directed toward children under 13. As the App collects no personal information and only processes audio locally, it does not knowingly collect any data from children.

11) Changes to this policy

If this Privacy Policy is updated, the new version will be published in the App Store release notes with an updated effective date. Continued use of the App after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
